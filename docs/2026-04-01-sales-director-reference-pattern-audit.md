## Reusable Patterns

1. Answer-first summary pages stay sparse.
The strongest decks lead with a direct business answer, then support it with a few high-signal proof blocks. They do not end important slides with a full-width dense action table unless the page is explicitly a queue view.

2. Proof-point pages beat dense lower tables.
`Commercial Update - EOY 2025` and `Sales Kickoff 2026 - CRO Keynote` repeatedly use a headline plus a few bold proof points like `Added +60 new logos` or `Scorecard delivery in 2025` instead of stuffing every fact into a lower grid.

3. QBR structure is sectioned and intentional.
`EMEA Q1-2026 QBR - Commercial` uses explicit `Fly-in`, `Run`, `Change`, and `P&C` framing. The value to borrow is not the agenda itself, but the clearer section intent and the consistent “what works / what needs to improve / action” rhythm.

4. Key-deal pages surface only the deciding items.
The commercial update and ExCo decks keep recurring focus on the handful of deals that change the quarter. They do not try to show every live item on the core executive pages.

5. Bridges are common, but not the right default for this monthly deck.
Recent commercial decks use ARR and revenue bridges heavily, but that density works because those packs are broader commercial-update decks. Report 1 should stay closer to “coverage, renewal, churn, and slipped-deal decision support” than to a finance bridge pack.

## What Not To Copy

- Do not import the full commercial-update bridge density into the monthly Sales Director pack.
- Do not reuse raw export tables as the bottom half of every page.
- Do not let QBR section language turn the deck into a workshop agenda instead of a monthly leadership readout.

## Ported Into The Current Baseline

The proven patterns now ported into the live baseline are:
- convert the renewals slide lower section from a dense month-action table into three quarter-critical proof cards
- replace the missing-Finance churn side table with observed churn proof points

These changes match the stronger SimCorp references better because the deck now lands on the few proof points that change the management read instead of ending important pages on compressed export tables or wrapped bullet text.
